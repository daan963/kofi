Here you will get all the current information in Saddle Bronc Riding for 2021 World Standings.

The first spot on the top of the leaderboard is Wyatt Casper, with a significant margin of over $33,000. The funny thing is that this will be his first significant qualification in NFR since he joined PRCA way back in 2015. Ryder Wright follows Casper at position two.

We had some significant upsets in the NFR when Stetson Wright, the 2019 All-Around World Champion, had to work his way up last month to secure an NFR qualification. Similarly, Zeke Thurston, the 2019 Saddle Bronc World Champion, only got position nine after being victorious in the Gold Buckle last season. In the Gold Buckle Knockout, we saw Taos Muncy ahead with exactly $1,793.35 over his competitor, Cody DeMoss. It’s just a matter of time since we can see the top Bareback and Saddle Bronc Riders in the world right now take up the mantle. However, the Gold Buckle Knockout’s final standings are expected to be released by Wednesday, Sept 30.
